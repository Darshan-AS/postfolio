# Session 6: Refactoring Deposit Status and Maturity Urgency

## Changes Made
- **Domain Models**: Refactored the `DepositStatus` enum by removing the redundant `matured` state. Now, the deposit lifecycle is strictly defined by binary contractual states: `active` and `closed`.
- **Domain Models**: Renamed `MaturityUrgency.overdue` to `MaturityUrgency.matured` to better reflect standard banking terminology without creating cognitive overload (since "overdue" implies debt).
- **Search Criteria**: Updated `RDSearchCriteria` and `OTDSearchCriteria` default status filters to use only `DepositStatus.active`.
- **Controllers**: Updated `clearFilters()` in both controllers to fallback to `DepositStatus.active` only.
- **UI & Presentation**: 
  - Adjusted the urgency filters to expose `matured` instead of `overdue`.
  - Removed logic checking for `DepositStatus.matured` from all detail, list, and form screens.
  - Refined status badge and indicator colors in `detail_components.dart`, `one_time_deposit_card.dart`, and `recurring_deposit_card.dart` to match the newly renamed `matured` urgency.
- **Mock Data**: Updated `FakeDataSource` to only ever assign deposits to `active` or `closed` statuses randomly, maintaining realism via their derived maturity timeline state.
- **Internationalization**: Updated localization map values in `en.i18n.yaml` and re-ran SLANG.
- **Code Generation**: Re-ran `build_runner` to rebuild `freezed` models and json serializers to apply enum changes.
