# Session Log - April 19, 2026 (Session 2)

## Purpose
Refine the documentation structure, explicitly separate software requirements from domain knowledge, and document the Indian Post Office mathematical formulas for accurate future implementations.

## Changes

- **Product Requirements Document (`docs/product_requirements.md`)**:
  - Removed heavy domain knowledge sections.
  - Added "Out of Scope" explicitly stating no direct server integration and no digital payment processing.
  - Added "Security & Authentication" detailing Email/Phone(OTP) Auth and local App Lock plans.
  - Aligned the document with `.agents/tasks.md` by adding Search & Filtering requirements to deposit tracking.
- **Domain Knowledge Extraction**:
  - Created `docs/domain_knowledge/business_context.md` to hold the Post Office Agency workflow and general scheme descriptions.
  - Created `docs/domain_knowledge/calculations.md` to document the precise mathematical formulas for maturity dates, maturity amounts, agent commissions, TDS deductions (accounting for the Oct 2024 2% TDS rule), late fees, early closures, and advance deposit rebates.

### Project Roadmap Updates
- **`tasks.md`**: 
  - Explicitly added Email and Phone (OTP) authentication to Phase 6.
  - Added App Lock (Biometrics/PIN) to Phase 7.

## Decisions Made
- **Documentation Modularity**: Strict separation between "What we are building" (PRD) and "The business rules we are modeling" (Domain Knowledge). This keeps the PRD actionable and the domain knowledge encyclopedic.
- **Formulas as Pure Logic**: Confirmed the architectural decision that all Post Office calculations documented in `calculations.md` must be implemented as pure Dart extension methods in the domain layer, not stored in the database.
