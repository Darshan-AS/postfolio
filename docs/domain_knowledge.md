# Domain Knowledge: Post Office Agency Business

## Background Context
In many developing nations, specifically modeled after the **Indian Post Office Small Savings Schemes**, postal networks operate as the primary banking institution for rural and middle-class populations. 

To mobilize these savings, the government issues **Agency Licenses** to individuals who act as intermediaries between the depositors and the Post Office. 

A prominent example of this is the **Mahila Pradhan Kshetriya Bachat Yojana (MPKBY)** (meaning: Women's Primary Regional Savings Scheme) in India.
* **Target Demographic:** MPKBY licenses are granted **exclusively to women**. This initiative is designed to empower women financially while encouraging savings habits in households.
* **Other Agent Types:** The **Standardised Agency System (SAS)** is another license type (open to all) for selling specific certificates and time deposits.

## The Agent's Role & Workflow
An agent's primary job is to canvas for deposits, collect the money from the customer's doorstep or workplace, and deposit it into the Post Office.

**The Workflow:**
1. **Acquisition:** The agent convinces a customer to open a savings account (e.g., a Recurring Deposit).
2. **Collection:** The agent visits the customer regularly (daily, weekly, or monthly) to collect cash or cheques.
3. **Record Keeping:** The agent updates their personal ledger and takes the customer's physical passbook.
4. **Deposit:** The agent visits the Post Office, fills out deposit slips (often called "schedules" or "ASLAAS cards"), deposits the collected cash, and gets the passbooks stamped.
5. **Return:** The agent returns the updated passbook to the customer.

## The Business Model (Commissions)
Agents do not charge the customers. They are compensated by the government/Post Office via a commission percentage on the deposits they mobilize.
* **Recurring Deposits (RD):** Typically yields a higher commission (e.g., **4%** of the collected deposit amount). This is the bread and butter for MPKBY agents.
* **Time Deposits / Certificates:** Typically yields a lower, one-time commission (e.g., **0.5% to 1%** of the lump sum amount).

## Core Pain Points (Why this app exists)
1. **Manual Ledgers:** Agents track hundreds of customers and thousands of micro-transactions in physical diaries.
2. **Collection Defaults:** Forgetting who has paid and who hasn't for the current month leads to missed deposits and penalty fees for the customer (which damages the agent's reputation).
3. **Commission Tracking:** Agents struggle to project their monthly earnings and reconcile whether the Post Office paid them the correct commission.
4. **Physical Burden:** Generating reports, adding up cash collected on a given day to ensure it matches the wallet, and filling out Post Office deposit schedules is highly prone to human error.
